# Code of Conduct

## Preamble

The **S.T.A.R. Program** (Symbolic–Topological–Arithmetic–Relativity) is an open scientific research project dedicated to advancing the frontiers of mathematical and theoretical physics through rigorous, reproducible, and collaborative inquiry. We are committed to fostering an inclusive, respectful, and intellectually honest environment for all participants.

## Our Values

We pledge to uphold the highest standards of scientific integrity, mutual respect, and constructive collaboration. Participation in this project — whether through code, theory, data analysis, documentation, or discussion — must align with the principles of ethical research and professional conduct.

## Expected Behavior

All contributors, maintainers, and participants are expected to:

- Treat others with courtesy, respect, and fairness
- Engage in constructive, evidence-based scientific discourse
- Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- Provide and accept constructive criticism with grace
- Give appropriate credit for intellectual contributions
- Maintain transparency and reproducibility in research

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable:

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation based on age, gender, sexual orientation, disability, ethnicity, nationality, religion, or any other personal characteristic
- Scientific misconduct, including fabrication, falsification, or plagiarism of results or ideas
- Intentionally disruptive or disrespectful behavior toward other contributors
- Public or private personal attacks
- Retaliation against individuals who report misconduct

## Scientific Integrity and Research Ethics

As a scientific project, we maintain the highest standards of research ethics. Contributors must:

- Ensure all theoretical claims, computational results, and data analyses are accurate and reproducible
- Clearly distinguish between established results, conjectures, and preliminary findings
- Properly cite prior work and acknowledge contributions from others
- Avoid selective reporting or misrepresentation of results
- Disclose potential conflicts of interest when relevant

Violations of scientific integrity (such as data manipulation, plagiarism, or unfounded claims presented as proven) will be treated with particular seriousness and may result in immediate removal from the project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct. They have the right to:

- Edit, remove, or reject contributions that violate these standards
- Issue warnings or temporary bans for repeated or serious violations
- Permanently ban individuals whose behavior is deemed incompatible with the project’s values

All reports will be handled confidentially and investigated fairly. Reports should be directed to the project maintainer(s) via GitHub private message or email.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, Jupyter notebooks, documentation, and any official project-related communication channels.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org) (version 2.1) and incorporates principles from established scientific codes of conduct (e.g., those of the American Physical Society and Committee on Publication Ethics).
